A medical device is any product used to diagnose, cure, or treat a condition, or to prevent disease. They range from small and simple, like a blood glucose meter, to large and complicated, like a ventilator. You might use one at home or at work, or you may need one in a hospital. To use medical devices safely: Know how your device works. Keep instructions close by Understand and properly respond to device alarms Have a back-up plan and supplies in the event of an emergency Keep emergency numbers available and update them as needed Educate your family and caregivers about your device Food and Drug Administration
